# ERP REPORTING MODULE SPECIFICATION

## AI-Powered ERP & CRM for Medical Equipment Suppliers, Hospitals & Biomedical Engineering Companies

---

# 1. PURPOSE

The Reporting Module is the centralized business intelligence component of the ERP.

Its objective is to enable management to make informed decisions through accurate, real-time, exportable reports generated from every department.

All reports are generated dynamically based on user-selected filters and are exportable to **Microsoft Excel (.xlsx)**.

# MARKET PROBLEMS THIS REPORTING MODULE SOLVES

## Poor Visibility

Managers obtain a real-time operational overview instead of relying on scattered spreadsheets.

## Inventory Losses

Variance, expiry, and stock movement reports expose shrinkage, overstocking, and slow-moving inventory.

## Missed Preventive Maintenance

Automated preventive maintenance reports reduce costly equipment failures and improve customer retention.

## Weak Sales Tracking

Salesperson, customer, and product reports identify revenue opportunities and underperforming segments.

## Delayed Decision-Making

Real-time reports eliminate manual data consolidation and enable faster management decisions.

## Financial Leakages

Accounts receivable, payment aging, and audit reports improve cash flow and accountability.

## Regulatory Compliance

Audit trails, service histories, warranty records, and installation documentation support healthcare quality standards and customer audits.

## Poor Technician Accountability

Engineer performance reports measure workload, response times, first-time fix rates, and service quality.

## Customer Retention

Equipment lifecycle reports, warranty tracking, and service histories strengthen after-sales support and contract renewals.

# FUTURE AI FEATURES

The reporting engine should be designed to support AI capabilities such as:

* Natural-language report generation (e.g., "Show breakdown services completed last month in Nairobi.")
* Automatic anomaly detection for unusual sales or inventory movements.
* Predictive stock replenishment using historical consumption trends.
* Preventive maintenance forecasting based on equipment service history.
* Revenue and cash-flow forecasting.
* Executive summaries generated automatically for management meetings.
* AI-powered recommendations highlighting low-performing products, overdue customer payments, and high-risk equipment requiring attention.

The reporting architecture should be built as a shared reporting engine so that every ERP module contributes data to a centralized analytics layer, ensuring consistency, scalability, and easier maintenance as new modules are added.
